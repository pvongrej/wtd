Manufacturers and ecosystems

Brands

This page looks at the brands behind the phones I have owned. Some of them were only a short stop in my phone history, while others became part of my everyday life for many years.

Each brand had a different role, from simple GSM phones and reliable button devices to Android experiments and modern Samsung flagships.

Alcatel

First mobile phone

Alcatel was the starting point of my phone history. It was not about apps, cameras, or performance, but simply about having a mobile phone for calls and messages. Looking back, it feels basic, but at the time it was the device that started the whole journey.

Alcatel One Touch Club+

Ericsson

Compact GSM phones

Ericsson phones had a very different character from today’s smartphones. They were small, practical, and focused on the essentials. For me, this brand represents the classic GSM period, when design, size, and battery life mattered more than features.

Ericsson T10, Ericsson T28s

Nokia

Reliability and everyday use

Nokia was probably the brand that best represented reliable button phones. These devices felt strong, simple, and easy to trust. They were not trying to do everything, but what they did, they usually did very well.

Nokia 6310i, Nokia 6230

Sony Ericsson

Style and multimedia

Sony Ericsson felt more playful and more focused on multimedia. It was the kind of brand that made phones feel a bit more personal, especially with better screens, cameras, music features, and a stronger focus on design.

Sony Ericsson K610i

HP

Pocket computer experiment

HP was the unusual one in the list. The iPAQ felt less like a normal phone and more like a small computer that happened to be portable. It was a good example of the period when companies were still trying to figure out what mobile computing should look like.

HP iPAQ hx4700

HTC

Android with character

HTC was my first real step into Android. What I liked about it was that the phone had its own style and did not feel completely generic. It showed me that a phone could be much more than a tool for calls and messages.

HTC One V

Google

Clean Android

Google was about Android in its cleanest form. The Nexus 5 felt simple, fast, and direct, without too many extras from the manufacturer. It was the phone that made the software experience feel more important than the brand logo.

Google Nexus 5

Samsung

Main smartphone brand

Samsung became the main brand in my smartphone history. I stayed with the Galaxy S series for several upgrades, mostly because the phones felt familiar, powerful, and reliable. Over time, Samsung also became harder to replace, because the whole experience around the phone became more polished.

Galaxy S6, S9, S20, S21, S24
